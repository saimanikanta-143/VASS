Snooker Rules & Regulations

Basic Rules

  • Objective: The goal is to score more points than the opponent by potting balls in the correct sequence.
  • Balls: There are 21 balls: 15 red (1 point each) and 6 colored balls with different values.
  • Turns: Players take turns attempting to pot balls.
  • Sequence: A red ball must be potted first, followed by a colored ball.
  • Fouls: Penalties occur for failing to hit a ball, potting the wrong ball, or touching the cue ball illegally.
  • Winning: The player with the most points at the end of the game wins.

Common Fouls

  • Failing to hit any ball.
  • Potting the cue ball (white ball).
  • Hitting the wrong ball first.
  • Touching the ball with anything other than the cue stick.
  • Failing to pot a ball on a legal shot.
