Tennis Rules & Regulations

Basic Rules

  • Objective: Players aim to hit the ball over the net and within the opponent’s court boundaries.
  • Scoring: Points are counted as 15, 30, 40, and game. A deuce occurs at 40-40, requiring two consecutive points to win.
  • Serving: The server must stand behind the baseline and serve diagonally into the opponent’s service box.
  • Faults: A serve that misses the correct service box is a fault. Two consecutive faults result in a double fault and loss of point.
  • Ball in Play: The ball must land within the court boundaries; otherwise, it is considered out.
  • Let: A serve that touches the net but lands in the correct service box is replayed.

Common Violations

  • Foot fault (stepping on or over the baseline while serving).
  • Double bounce (ball bounces twice before returning it).
  • Out of bounds (hitting the ball outside court boundaries).
  • Illegal racket contact (touching the net or using hands).
  • Hindrance (intentionally distracting the opponent).
